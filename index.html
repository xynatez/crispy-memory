About the Lab

A student-initiated research group at Universitas Indonesia, dedicated to addressing complex, real-world challenges through interdisciplinary inquiry. The lab explores unsolved problems and develops human-centered solutions with academic rigor and global relevance.

The 2025 Membership Program

A rare and rigorous program designed to bring together minds that think across boundaries. Open to exceptional high school students, undergraduates, master's students, and PhD candidates from any university worldwide. This is the one and only membership intake for 2025.

Our Mission

Membership Criteria

We seek anomalies through two distinct pathways.

Standard Path

For candidates who fulfill all three requirements.

  • Official IQ Score > 140: Documented results from recognized assessments (Mensa, WAIS-IV, etc.).
  • Mastery of 3+ Disciplines: At least one from the Core Six (Maths, CS, Physics, Medicine, Psychology, Econ). Evidence required.
  • History of Original Thought: Demonstrable creation of frameworks, systems, or independent research.

Special Consideration: Candidates meeting 2 of 3 requirements are still encouraged to apply. We review applications holistically.

Exceptional Path

For those who have redefined the boundaries of what's possible.

This path waives IQ and multi-domain requirements if you have:

Solved a revolutionary problem, made a measurable societal contribution through innovation, or created breakthrough solutions with real-world impact. Comprehensive evidence of achievement is required.

CRYPTOGRAPHY & SECURITY

IAST: Irreversible Alwin-Sahira Transform

A lightweight, one-way function for data integrity and key derivation, forming the basis of a GeoFence protocol that restricts decryption to specific geographical locations.

TYPE: Technical Report - June 2025

THEORETICAL COMPUTER SCIENCE

Complexity Calculus: A Framework for P vs NP

Introduces a novel geometric-entropic mathematical framework designed to tackle the P vs NP conjecture, one of the most profound open problems in computer science.

TYPE: Research Proposal - February 2025

COMPUTATIONAL BIOLOGY

An analysis of the computational complexity of protein folding, focusing on the NP-hardness of structure prediction using the HP lattice model.
